Basic Grip

The correct grip is essential for control and power. Hold the cane about one fist away from the base (the punyo). Your fingers should wrap firmly around the stick, with your thumb overlapping your index and middle fingers. Keep your wrist relaxed but firm.

Basic Fighting Stance

A good stance provides balance and mobility. Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart. Step your dominant foot forward slightly. Bend your knees to lower your center of gravity. Keep your weapon hand up and ready, and your live hand (empty hand) close to your chest for checking and blocking.

The 12 Striking Techniques

Modern Arnis utilizes a standardized 12-strike system. Here is the breakdown of the target areas:

1. Left Temple Strike: Forehand strike to the opponent's left temple.
2. Right Temple Strike: Backhand strike to the opponent's right temple.
3. Left Shoulder Strike: Forehand strike to the left shoulder.
4. Right Shoulder Strike: Backhand strike to the right shoulder.
5. Stomach Thrust: Straight thrust to the midsection.
6. Left Chest Thrust: Thrust to the left side of the chest.
7. Right Chest Thrust: Thrust to the right side of the chest.
8. Right Knee Strike: Backhand strike to the lower leg/knee.
9. Left Knee Strike: Forehand strike to the lower leg/knee.
10. Left Eye Poke: Forward thrust targeting the left eye.
11. Right Eye Poke: Forward thrust targeting the right eye.
12. Crown Strike: Downward strike to the top of the head.

Basic Blocking Techniques

Defending against incoming strikes is just as important as attacking. The primary blocks include:

  • Inside Block: Defends against strikes to the left side of the body (e.g., Strike 1). The cane is held vertically, moving from right to left.
  • Outside Block: Defends against strikes to the right side of the body (e.g., Strike 2). The cane moves from left to right.
  • Downward Block: Used to defend against strikes to the lower body (knees). Can be performed forehand or backhand.
  • Roof / Umbrella Block: Protects the head from downward strikes (e.g., Strike 12). The cane is held horizontally above the head.

Basic Footwork

In Arnis, footwork is the foundation of evasion and generating power. We utilize a triangular framework for movement.

Forward Triangle

Stepping forward at a 45-degree angle to the left or right to evade direct strikes while closing the distance to counter-attack.

Reverse Triangle

Stepping backward at a 45-degree angle to create distance and draw the opponent in.
